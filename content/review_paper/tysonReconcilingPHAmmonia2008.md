#### Abstract
>Integrating hydroponic and aquaculture systems (aquaponics) requires balanced pH for plants, ï¬sh, and nitrifying bacteria. Nitriï¬cation prevents accumulation of ï¬sh waste ammonia by converting it to NO3â€“-N. The difference in optimum pH for hydroponic cucumber (Cucumis sativa) (5.5 to 6.0) and nitriï¬cation (7.5 to 9.0) requires reconciliation to improve systems integration and sustainability. The purpose of this investigation was to: 1) determine the ammonia bioï¬ltration rate of a perlite trickling bioï¬lter/root growth medium in an aquaponic system, 2) predict the relative contribution of nitriï¬ers and plants to ammonia bioï¬ltration, and 3) establish the reconciling pH for ammonia bioï¬ltration and cucumber yield in recirculating aquaponics. The bioï¬ltration rate of total ammonia nitrogen (TAN) removal was 19, 31, and 80 gÃmâ€“3Ãdâ€“1 for aquaponic systems [cucumber, tilapia (Oreochromis niloticus), and nitrifying bacteria (Nitrosomonas sp. + Nitrobacter sp.)] with operating pH at 6.0, 7.0, and 8.0, respectively. With the existing aquaponic design (four plants/20 L perlite bioï¬lter/100 L tank water), the aquaponic bioï¬lter (with plants and nitriï¬ers) was three times more effective at removing TAN compared with plant uptake alone at pH 6.0. Most probable number of Nitrosomonas sp. bacteria cells sampled from bioï¬lter cores indicated that the aquaculture control (pH 7.0) had a signiï¬cantly higher (0.01% level) bacteria cell number compared with treatments containing plants in the bioï¬lter (pH 6.0, 7.0, or 8.0). However, the highest TAN removal was with aquaponic production at pH 8.0. Thus, operating pH was more important than nitrifying bacteria population in determining the rate of ammonia bioï¬ltration. Early marketable cucumber fruit yield decreased linearly from 1.5 to 0.7 kg/plant as pH increased from 6.0 to 8.0, but total marketable yield was not different. The reconciling pH for this system was pH 8.0, except during production for early-season cucumber market windows in which pH 7.0 would be recommended.
